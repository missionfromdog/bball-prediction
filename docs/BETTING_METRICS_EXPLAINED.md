## üìä Edge: +8.2%

**What it means:**
- This is the difference between your model's prediction and the bookmaker's "fair" probability (after removing the house edge).
- **+8.2%** means your model thinks the team has an **8.2 percentage points higher** chance of winning than what the bookmaker's odds suggest.

**In plain English:**
- Your model: "This team has a 68.2% chance to win"

- **Edge = 68.2% - 60% = +8.2%**

- ‚úÖ **Positive edge** (green): Your model sees value - the bookmaker is underestimating this team

## üí∞ EV (Expected Value): $0.11

**What it means:**
- This is how much money you expect to **profit per $1 bet** if you make this bet many times.
- **$0.11** means for every $1 you bet, you expect to make **11 cents profit** on average over the long run.

**In plain English:**
- If you bet $1 on this game 100 times, you'd expect to make about **$11 profit** total (after accounting for wins and losses)
- This is a **positive EV bet** - it's profitable in the long run

**How to use it:**
- ‚úÖ **Positive EV** (green): Profitable bet - make this bet
- ‚ùå **Negative EV** (red): Losing bet - avoid this bet
- The higher the EV, the more profitable the bet

**Your example ($0.11):**
This is a **positive EV bet** - you expect to make money over time. Not huge, but consistently positive EV bets add up!

**Real-world example:**

## üéØ Kelly: 12.5%

**What it means:**
- This is the **optimal percentage of your bankroll** you should bet on this game, according to the Kelly Criterion formula.
- **12.5%** means you should bet **12.5% of your total bankroll** on this game.

**In plain English:**
- If your bankroll is $500, bet 12.5% = **$62.50** on this game
- The Kelly Criterion maximizes your long-term growth while protecting your bankroll

**How to use it:**
- ‚úÖ **0-10%**: Conservative bet size - safe and recommended
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **10-20%**: Moderate bet size - still reasonable
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **20-25%**: Aggressive bet size - higher risk
- ‚ùå **0% or negative**: Don't bet - no value or negative EV

**Your example (12.5%):**
This is a **moderate bet size** - not too conservative, not too aggressive. It's a good balance between growth and risk management.

**Why Kelly matters:**
- Betting too much: Risk of ruin (losing your bankroll)
- Betting too little: Missing out on growth
- Kelly finds the "sweet spot" that maximizes long-term profits

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why is my bet size so high/low?**
A: Bet size depends on your bankroll and the Kelly percentage. Increase/decrease your bankroll in the sidebar to adjust.

**Q: What if Kelly says to bet 25% but that's too much?**
A: Kelly is capped at 25% for safety. If that's still too much, you can manually bet less, but you'll be leaving some value on the table.

**Q: Can I bet more than Kelly recommends?**
A: Not recommended. Kelly is mathematically optimal - betting more increases risk without improving long-term returns.

**Q: What if EV is positive but edge is negative?**
A: This shouldn't happen mathematically. If it does, there may be an error in the calculations.

**Q: How often will I win with positive EV bets?**
A: You won't win every time! EV is about long-term averages. A +$0.11 EV bet might lose 3 times in a row, but over 100 bets, you'll be profitable.
